TTSVowelViz: A Tool for Visualising Text-to-Speech Model Training via Vowel Spaces

Pasindu Udawatta, Jesin James, B.T. Balamurali, Catherine Watson, Ake Nicholas, Binu Abeysinghe
LREC 2026 • Language Resources and Evaluation Conference • Palma, Spain

Introduces TTSVowelViz, a tool that visualises vowel spaces during text-to-speech model training, bridging linguistic knowledge and AI development. It tracks how vowel sounds are learned over time, revealing linguistic characteristics and improving interpretability beyond traditional loss curves.

Pasindu Udawatta, Indunil Udayangana, Chathulanka Gamage, Ravi Shekhar, Surangika Ranathunga
World Wide Web • Volume 27, Article Number 63

Explores code-mixed and code-switched text classification using prompt-based learning and introduces a novel approach which combines script-aware modeling with task-specific adapters. Experiments show that proposed method outperforms standard fine-tuning and prompting techniques.

TTSVowelViz: A Tool for Visualising Text-to-Speech Model Training Through Vowel Spaces

Pasindu Udawatta, Jesin James, B.T. Balamurali, Catherine Watson, Ake Nicholas, Binu Abeysinghe
ICASSP 2025 - Show & Tell • IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing • Hyderabad, India

TTSVowelViz is an interactive tool that visualises vowel spaces in real-time during text-to-speech model training. By mapping vowel sounds using formant frequencies, it helps to identify which sounds are accurately learned, enabling early detection of issues without costly retraining.

Presentation 2025 Nov 28

Can Vowel Space Visualisation Reveal How Text-to-Speech Systems Learn?

Pasindu Udawatta, Jesin James, Catherine Watson, Ake Nicholas, B.T. Balamurali
LingSoc 2025 • Conference of the Linguistic Society of New Zealand • Wellington, New Zealand

Proposes using vowel space visualisation to evaluate how well text-to-speech systems learn vowel sounds. Results show that as the system develops, its vowel production becomes more similar to real speech, aligning with human perception and demonstrating the method’s effectiveness for evaluating speech learning.
